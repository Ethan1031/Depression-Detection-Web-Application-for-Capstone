Security

Effective Date: 6th March 2025

Last Updated: 6th March 2025

At MindLoom, protecting your data and ensuring the security of our platform is our top priority. This page outlines our security measures, compliance standards, and best practices.

1. Data Encryption

All user data, including PHQ-9 test results and medical insights, is encrypted using industry-standard AES-256 encryption.

2. Secure Authentication

  • We use two-factor authentication (2FA) for enhanced login security.
  • Passwords are stored using bcrypt hashing.
  • Account activity logs are monitored to detect unauthorized access.

3. Compliance Standards

MindLoom follows security compliance frameworks, including:

4. Data Access and User Control

You have full control over your personal data:

  • View, update, or delete your data through your profile settings.
  • Request data export in compliance with PDPA.
  • Opt out of non-essential data collection.

5. Threat Detection and Monitoring

We actively monitor for potential cyber threats:

  • 24/7 security monitoring and intrusion detection.
  • Automated alerts for suspicious login attempts.
  • Regular penetration testing and security audits.

7. Data Retention Policy

MindLoom retains personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in our Privacy Policy. We securely delete or anonymize data after:

  • Inactive accounts: 2 years from the last login date.
  • PHQ-9 test results: 1 year after submission unless required for medical purposes.
  • Financial transactions: 5 years to comply with legal and tax obligations.

If you request account deletion, your personal data will be erased within 30 days, except where legally required.

8. Security Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting your personal data, MindLoom will:

  • Notify affected users via email within 72 hours.
  • Take immediate action to contain and mitigate the breach.
  • Report serious breaches to the **Personal Data Protection Department Malaysia (JPDP)** as required by law.

9. Report a Security Issue

MindLoom takes security seriously. If you encounter a security issue, suspect unauthorized access, or experience a data privacy concern, please report it immediately.
